1. Tabular Data

For data in spreadsheets or databases, the trade-off is often between the raw power of complex models and the interpretability of simpler ones. Gradient Boosting models frequently hit the sweet spot.

This chart compares model families on a scale of performance versus their simplicity and speed. Simple models are great starting points, while deep learning excels with massive datasets.

2. Image Data

For visual tasks, Convolutional Neural Networks (CNNs) are king. The choice depends on your specific goal, from simple classification to generating entirely new images.

This radar chart shows the relative strengths of different model types for image tasks. Transfer learning is highly efficient for small datasets, while generative models are computationally intensive.

3. Text Data

Natural Language Processing has been revolutionized by Transformer models. While older models are still useful for simple tasks, Transformers offer state-of-the-art understanding and generation capabilities.

This bubble chart visualizes text model families by performance and complexity. The size of the bubble represents the typical data size required for good results.

4. Sequence & Temporal Data

When order matters, such as in time-series forecasting or DNA analysis, specialized architectures are needed to capture dependencies over time.

This chart illustrates model suitability based on the complexity and length of a data sequence. Classical models work well for simple patterns, while Transformers excel at capturing long-range dependencies.
